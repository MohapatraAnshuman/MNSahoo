Life & Journey

From a zamindar family in Niali to the summit of Odia literature

Mohapatra Nilamani Sahoo was born on 22 December 1926 in Niali, Cuttack District, Odisha, into a wealthy zamindar (landlord) family. Despite his aristocratic upbringing, he devoted his life to literature, education, and the exploration of human nature through the written word. He completed his matriculation from Binjharpur High School and went on to earn his graduation and post-graduation degrees from the prestigious Ravenshaw College, Cuttack, specialising in Odia language and literature.

His literary career began in the early 1950s and spanned over six decades, during which he published more than 55 works including short story collections, novels, literary criticism, essays, translations, and spiritual writings. He was widely regarded as one of the finest Odia short story writers of the post-independence era.

Beyond writing, Sahoo served the academic world with distinction. He worked as a librarian at Sambalpur University, taught Odia at Bhadrak College and Paralakhemundi Government College, and eventually rose to become Vice-Principal and then Principal of BJB Evening College, Bhubaneswar.

As an editor, he guided the literary magazine Jhankara (1965–1969) and later edited Utkala Prasanga and Orissa Review (1975–1978), influential periodicals that shaped Odia literary discourse.

Mohapatra Nilamani Sahoo passed away on 25 June 2016 in Bhubaneswar at the age of 89. He is survived by his wife Prasanna Kumari Devi, and his legacy endures through his son Guruprasad Mohapatra (1962–2021), a distinguished IAS officer who translated his father's stories into English.

Literary Style

A master of the human condition

  • Described the subtleties of human emotion with verbal and situational comedy
  • Known for satirising human idiocies with warmth and insight
  • Blended realism with romance and existential thought
  • Candid expression covering youthful love and human relationships
  • Explored complexities of social dynamics and tradition versus modernity
  • Wrote extensively on the philosophies of Sri Aurobindo
  • Gave talks on Aurobindo, Ramakrishna Paramhansa, and Swami Vivekananda
  • Translated Sri Aurobindo's Savitri into Odia
His writings explored the interplay between tradition and modernity, capturing the essence of Odia life and the universal complexities of human relationships with an unmatched candour and literary grace.
— On the literary legacy of Mohapatra Nilamani Sahoo

A Son's Tribute

Guruprasad Mohapatra (22 April 1962 – 19 June 2021), a 1986-batch Gujarat cadre IAS officer who rose to become Secretary of the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Government of India, carried forward his father's literary legacy by translating his stories into English. The collection, The Best Stories of Mohapatra Nilamani Sahoo, was published by Sahitya Akademi in 2021. Guruprasad was posthumously awarded the Padma Shri in 2022 for his contributions during the COVID pandemic. Two films based on Sahoo's stories have received National Film Awards.
